Model: each bet wins with prob p returning b per $1 staked (net odds from entry price), else loses the stake. Sizing per strategy: fixed-fraction, compound, ¼-Kelly, or all-in. Edge/bet = p·(1+b) − 1. Monte Carlo runs the configured paths × horizon with a seeded PRNG. This is a model, not a backtest — favorite-buying is modelled near-fair (thin edge, as the data shows), sharp/front-run strategies carry a small positive edge, market-making earns spread at low variance. Real results depend on copy latency, slippage, liquidity, and whether the copied wallet's edge persists.
